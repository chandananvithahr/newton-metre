## 1. Indian Automobile Industry Overview (FY 2024-25)

### Production
- **Total production:** 3.10 crore vehicles (31 million), up from 2.84 crore in FY 2023-24 (+9.2%)
- Includes: Passenger Vehicles, Commercial Vehicles, Three Wheelers, Two Wheelers, Quadricycles

### Economic Contribution
- Industry contribution to Indian economy: **>₹20 lakh crore** (~$240B)
- GST contribution: **₹3.37 lakh crore** (~15% of India's total GST collections)
- Motor Vehicle Tax: **₹0.88 lakh crore**
- India GDP growth: 6.5% in FY 2024-25

## 6. Relevance to Costimize Cost Estimation

### Market Size Context
| Metric | Value | Implication |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Total vehicles produced | 31M/year | Massive component demand |
| PV production | ~4.3M/year | Each PV has 20,000-30,000 parts |
| CV production | ~1M/year | Heavy machined components |
| Industry revenue | ₹20L Cr | Component manufacturing ~40% = ₹8L Cr |

### Component Manufacturing Implications
- **Turned parts demand:** Engine shafts, transmission gears, suspension parts → 31M vehicles × 50-100 turned parts each = **1.5-3 billion turned parts/year**
- **Sheet metal demand:** Body panels, brackets, structural parts → largest volume category
- **Machined parts:** Engine blocks, cylinder heads, gearbox housings → high-value precision parts
- **Surface treatments:** All vehicles need corrosion protection → zinc plating, e-coat, powder coating at massive scale
- **Heat treatments:** Gears, shafts, springs → carburizing, induction hardening at scale

### Regional Manufacturing Hubs (from SIAM industry context)
| Region | Key OEMs/Clusters | Specialization |
|--------|-------------------|---------------|
| Chennai (TN) | Hyundai, Renault-Nissan, BMW, Daimler | PV, CV, export hub |
| Pune (MH) | Tata, Mercedes, Bajaj, Force | PV, CV, 2W, defense |
| Gurugram-Manesar (HR) | Maruti Suzuki, Hero | PV, 2W |
| Ahmedabad-Sanand (GJ) | Tata Nano plant, Suzuki | PV |
| Bengaluru (KA) | Toyota, Volvo | PV, CV |
| Pithampur (MP) | Force Motors, MAN | CV, tractors |
| Jamshedpur (JH) | Tata Motors | CV (oldest plant) |
| Lucknow (UP) | Tata Motors | CV |
| Hosur (TN) | TVS, Ashok Leyland | 2W, CV |
| Dharwad (KA) | BEL, BEML | Defense vehicles |

### Cost Estimation Relevance
1. **Volume tiers matter:** A bracket for Maruti (400K/year) vs DRDO (500/year) has 100x cost difference
2. **Regional wage differentials:** Pune pays 20-25% more than Pithampur for same skill
3. **Export quality premium:** Parts for export need tighter tolerances, better surface finish → 15-30% cost premium
4. **EV transition:** New part types (battery housings, motor shafts) — aluminum machining and sheet metal growing vs steel
