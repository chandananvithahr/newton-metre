# Indian Regional Manufacturing Costs

Manufacturing costs in India vary by 25-50% depending on the city, driven by labour rates, machine age, power tariffs, real estate, and cluster specialization. This article consolidates data from government MSME tool rooms, state electricity commissions, and industry surveys across 15 major manufacturing cities.

## Labour Rates by City

CNC operator hourly rates (derived from annualized salaries) span a wide range across three tiers.

**Tier 1 cities (INR 200-360/hr):**
- Bangalore: INR 270-360/hr (highest; aerospace/defense premium)
- Pune: INR 245-315/hr (auto cluster demand)
- Chennai: INR 230-260/hr ("Detroit of India"; stable demand)
- Hyderabad: INR 210-250/hr (growing defense/pharma)
- Delhi-NCR (Manesar/Noida): INR 200-260/hr (Maruti/Hero belt)

**Tier 2 cities (INR 100-210/hr):**
- Ahmedabad: INR 165-215/hr
- Nashik: INR 158-210/hr (HAL/defense ecosystem)
- Coimbatore: INR 100-165/hr (motors/pumps cluster)
- Ludhiana: INR 110-175/hr (hand tools/bicycle/auto parts)
- Rajkot: INR 100-155/hr (pumps/valves; large MSME base)
- Kolhapur: INR 110-155/hr (foundry/forging cluster)
- Jamshedpur: INR 125-165/hr (Tata Steel/heavy engineering)

**Tier 3 (INR 95-140/hr):**
- Pithampur: INR 95-140/hr (lowest rates nationwide; MP auto hub)

### Government Tool Room Rates

Government MSME tool rooms offer subsidized rates that serve as a floor reference.

**MSME Tool Room Kolkata (2024-25):** CNC Turning INR 450/hr, CNC Milling INR 450-600/hr, Wire EDM INR 640/hr, Surface Grinding INR 225/hr, CNC 5-Axis INR 800/hr.

**CTR Ludhiana (2021):** CNC Turning INR 200/hr, CNC VMC INR 350-800/hr, Wire EDM INR 160-800/hr, Surface Grinding INR 125-300/hr. Micro-enterprise tier gets ~20% discount.

Private job shop rates run 1.5-2.5x government rates because government rates are subsidized, exclude tooling consumables, and carry lower overhead.

## Overhead and Factory Costs

Factory rental ranges from INR 8-14/sq.ft/month in Pithampur and Jamshedpur to INR 25-35/sq.ft/month in Chakan MIDC (Pune) and Manesar (Gurugram).

Combined overhead as percentage of direct manufacturing cost:
- Metro Tier 1 (high-cost parks): 18-25%
- Tier 2 city: 12-18%
- Tier 3/rural industrial: 8-14%

## Cost Spread Summary

The total cost spread between the cheapest (Pithampur/Jamshedpur) and most expensive (Bangalore/Pune) manufacturing locations is approximately 25-35% for equivalent work. The primary drivers are labour cost (30-50% cheaper in Tier 2), real estate (40-60% lower), and machine age (Tier 2 MSMEs often run older, fully amortized machines).

Newton-Metre's cost engine uses Pune/Maharashtra as the baseline (labour INR 250/hr, power INR 8/kWh, overhead 15%) and applies regional multipliers for location-adjusted estimates.
