# Indian Manufacturing Landscape

India's manufacturing sector is valued at USD 1.63 trillion (2025), projected to reach USD 2.47 trillion by 2031 at 7.26% CAGR. Manufacturing contributes approximately 17% of GDP. The MSME sector is the backbone: 71.6 million registered enterprises, contributing 30.1% of GDP, 35.4% of manufacturing output, and 45.73% of exports.

## Automobile Industry (SIAM Data FY 2024-25)

India is the world's 3rd largest automobile market, producing 31 million vehicles annually.

**Production and sales:**
- Total production: 3.10 crore vehicles (up 9.2% YoY)
- Passenger vehicles sold: 43.02 lakh (all-time high)
- Two-wheelers sold: 19.6 million
- Commercial vehicles sold: 9.57 lakh
- Three-wheelers: 7.4 lakh (crossed previous peak)
- Exports: 5.3 million+ vehicles (+14% value growth)

**Economic contribution:**
- Industry revenue: over INR 20 lakh crore (~USD 240B)
- GST contribution: INR 3.37 lakh crore (~15% of India's total GST)
- Component manufacturing accounts for approximately 40% = INR 8 lakh crore

**Component demand from 31M vehicles/year:**
- Turned parts: 1.5-3 billion parts/year (shafts, gears, suspension components)
- Sheet metal: largest volume category (body panels, brackets, structural parts)
- Surface treatments: every vehicle requires corrosion protection at massive scale
- Heat treatments: gears, shafts, springs require carburizing and induction hardening

**Key trends:** Passenger cars declining (shift to SUVs/UVs). UV segment now 65% of PV sales. EV transition creating new part types (battery housings, motor shafts) with growing aluminum machining and sheet metal demand versus steel.

## Defense and Aerospace Sector

Defense production reached INR 1.27 lakh crore in FY 2024-25. Defense corridors established in Uttar Pradesh (Lucknow-Kanpur-Agra-Aligarh-Chitrakoot-Jhansi) and Tamil Nadu (Chennai-Hosur-Salem-Coimbatore-Tiruchirappalli). Karnataka accounts for 65% of India's aerospace production value.

**Key defense facilities:**
- Avadi (Chennai): Arjun MBT, armoured vehicles
- Medak (Telangana): Gun carriages, armoured vehicles
- Khadki (Pune): Ammunition, explosives (Munitions India Ltd HQ)
- Nashik: HAL Aircraft Division (Su-30MKI, Tejas Mk1A)
- Bangalore: HAL HQ, ADA, NAL, ISRO (Tejas LCA, ALH Dhruv)
- Hyderabad: HAL Avionics, BDL (missile systems)

**Private defense hubs:** Pune-Nashik corridor (L&T Defense, Bharat Forge), Hyderabad (TATA Advanced Systems, Adani Defence), Bangalore (Dynamatic, Aequs, Alpha Design).

Defense indigenization mandate targets 75% self-reliance. Indian defense exports reached INR 21,083 Cr in FY24, up 30% YoY.

## Government Initiatives

**Make in India (2014):** Targets 25% manufacturing GDP share (currently ~17%). Simplified FDI norms, single-window clearances.

**PLI Schemes:** 14 sectors covered with INR 1.97 lakh crore outlay. Actual investment realized: INR 2.02 lakh crore. Production/sales: over INR 18.7 lakh crore incremental. Employment generated: 12.6 lakh.

**Digital MSME:** Government e-Marketplace (GeM) has 11.25 lakh MSE sellers with INR 7.44 lakh crore cumulative orders. SAMARTH Udyog promotes Industry 4.0 for MSMEs.

## The MSME Reality

95-97% of India's 71.6 million MSMEs are micro-enterprises (investment up to INR 1 crore, turnover up to INR 5 crore). These are small job shops with 5-50 employees, basic machinery, and minimal digital infrastructure.

- 97% cannot justify CAD software expense (AutoCAD costs INR 50K/yr)
- CAD piracy rate: 56%
- PDF is king: 60-70% of procurement drawings exchanged as PDF
- WhatsApp is the de facto drawing platform for MSMEs (India has 390M WhatsApp users)
- Drawing degradation chain: OEM (CATIA) to Tier 1 (PDF) to Tier 2 (scan) to Job shop (WhatsApp photo)

## Drawing Standards and Conventions

India's engineering drawing standards are governed by BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards), closely aligned with ISO.

**Key standards:**
- IS 10714: Technical drawings (lines, lettering, views) -- equivalent to ISO 128
- IS 11669: Dimensioning principles -- equivalent to ISO 129
- IS 2102: General tolerances -- equivalent to ISO 2768
- IS 8000: Geometrical tolerancing (GD&T) -- equivalent to ISO 1101

**India uses First Angle Projection** (ISO/European convention), unlike the US (Third Angle per ASME Y14.5). Shops working on export orders handle both conventions.

**Units:** Metric (mm for dimensions, Ra for surface finish). Mixed usage exists: older shops and those working with US clients use imperial.

**The N.D. Bhatt standard:** "Engineering Drawing" by N.D. Bhatt is the canonical textbook used in 90%+ of Indian engineering colleges. Every Indian mechanical engineer studied from this book. Drawings follow BIS conventions with First Angle Projection and metric units.

## BIS Steel Grades

Indian shops use a mix of naming systems: IS number (IS 2062 E250), old IS name (C45), British EN number (EN8, EN19, EN24 -- most commonly used on shop floors), and AISI/SAE (4140, 4340).

**IS 2062** (hot rolled structural steel) is the most common standard. E250 grade maps to "Mild Steel" in cost engines.

**Key cross-references:**
- IS 40Cr4 = EN 18 = AISI 5140
- IS 40Cr4Mo2 = EN 19 = AISI 4140 (very common)
- IS 103Cr4 = EN 31 = AISI 52100
- IS 15Cr13Ni3 = EN 36 = AISI 3310

## Drawing Format Distribution

| Format | Share | Context |
|--------|-------|---------|
| PDF (native/exported) | 35-40% | From OEMs and Tier-1 suppliers |
| PDF (scanned) | 25-30% | Legacy archives, defense organizations |
| DXF/DWG | 10-15% | Between companies with CAD capability |
| WhatsApp photos | 10-15% | Job shop to job shop, urgent quotes |
| STEP/IGES (3D) | 3-5% | Aerospace, automotive Tier-1 |
| Physical paper | 5-10% | Government tenders, older shops |

**Critical insight:** Procurement teams do NOT have CAD software. They receive drawings as PDFs or images and cannot measure dimensions. Their cost estimation relies on forwarding to technical staff, comparing historical quotes, or gut feeling. This is the exact gap that AI-powered should-cost estimation fills.

| Industry | Part Types | Volumes | Key Requirement |

| Defense | Turned, milled, sheet metal | Low (50-5,000) | 2D drawings, tight tolerances, BIS/MIL-STD |

Volume tiers produce 100x cost differences: a bracket for Maruti (400K/year) versus DRDO (500/year) requires entirely different cost models. Export-quality parts carry a 15-30% premium for tighter tolerances and better surface finish.
